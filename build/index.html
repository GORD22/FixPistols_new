FIXPISTOLS — Direct Fastening System

The FIXPISTOLS | Direct Fastening System is a method of fastening by special nails or studs to steel, concrete or brick work with a fastening tool (nailer). This promising method replaces the traditional drilling method in many areas of construction.

Power sources:

  • - Cartridge in the Powder-Actuated Technology
  • - Fuel cell in the Gas Technology
  • - Battery charge in the Battery Technology.

During the shot, the base material is deformed and compacted without fracture.

Advantages of Direct Fastening System:

  • - Very high speed of performance (up to twenty times higher)
  • - The cost of Direct Fastening is similar to the traditional technology (to drilling)
  • - Easy in use
  • - Weatherproof
  • - Autonomous operation without power supply
  • - No dust, low noise level
  • - Work in hard-to-reach areas.

The system components (fastening element, fastening tool and power source) form an installation system with its own specific characteristics.

The FIXPISTOLS DIRECT FASTENING SYSTEM is divided into POWDER-ACTUATED, GAS AND BATTERY TECHNOLOGIES according to the type of energy source used to generate the force transmitted to the fastening element.

The choice of technology and elements for reliable fastening depends on the assessment of the conditions to which the fastener is exposed, as well as on a correct calculation of the load-bearing capacity of the fastening element.

Necessary conditions for a predictable performance of the fastener:

  • - The fastening system must be designed for the specific application and tested accordingly
  • - The quality of the components of the fastening system used must match the quality of the originally tested components
  • - Fastenings must be made in the manner specified in the system technology or in the same manner as when the system was tested
  • - Minimum number of fastening points for critical mountings: at least 5.
